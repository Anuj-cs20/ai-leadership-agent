Following the October 28 Board Meeting and the Q3 performance review,
the executive team has agreed on the following priority directives,
effective immediately.

DIRECTIVE 1: CUSTOMER SUPPORT EMERGENCY STABILIZATION
Owner: VP of Customer Support
Deadline: Q1 2025
Budget: $4.1M (approved by Board)

Actions required:
- Fill all 18 open support positions by December 31, 2024
- Open 7 additional headcount requests for Q1 2025
- Implement 15% market-rate compensation adjustment for all support staff
  (effective January 1, 2025, $1.8M annual impact)
- Deploy AI ticket triage system by February 2025
  (vendor selection: Zendesk AI — contract under review)
- Establish weekly CSAT tracking dashboard (currently only monthly)
- Target: CSAT recovery to 75% by Q2 2025, 80% by Q4 2025

Current metrics:
  CSAT: 68% (target: 80%)
  Avg ticket resolution: 7.8 hours (target: <5 hours)
  Ticket backlog: 445 (target: <200)
  Staff turnover: 28% annualized
  Headcount: 94 (model requires: 120)

DIRECTIVE 2: MARKETING STRATEGY RESET
Owner: CMO
Deadline: Q1 2025
Budget: $2.4M incremental

Actions required:
- Reallocate 30% of paid search budget to Account-Based Marketing (ABM)
- Hire VP of Demand Generation by January 2025
- Complete CRM-marketing platform integration by end of Q1 2025
- Align with Sales on joint target account list (top 200 prospects)
- Shift to quarterly OKR reviews (from semi-annual)
- Target: MQL generation to reach 100% of quarterly target by Q2 2025

Current metrics:
  MQL attainment: 72% of annual target
  Paid search ROI: 2.8x (was 4.2x, benchmark: 4.0x)
  Content/SEO ROI: 5.1x (strongest channel)
  Marketing score: 58/100 (target: 80/100)

DIRECTIVE 3: COMPENSATION AND RETENTION
Owner: CHRO
Deadline: Q1 2025 (compensation review complete)
Budget: $6.2M annual

Actions required:
- Complete company-wide compensation benchmarking by December 2024
- Implement market-rate adjustments for engineering and support roles
  (estimated 8-12% increase for affected positions)
- Launch formal career ladder framework for all departments
- Mandatory leadership development program for all people managers
- Standardize hybrid work policy across all departments
- Target: Reduce organization-wide turnover from 23% to 16% by Q4 2025

Current metrics:
  Overall turnover: 23% (target: 15%)
  Engineering turnover: 19%
  Support turnover: 28%
  Engagement score: 3.2/5.0 (was 3.6/5.0)
  Exit interview top reason: "limited growth opportunities" (45%)

DIRECTIVE 4: SECURITY AND COMPLIANCE
Owner: CISO + CLO
Deadline: Q1 2025 (EU DSA), Q2 2025 (GDPR amendments)
Budget: $3.8M

Actions required:
- Complete penetration testing program (CyberShield Labs engaged)
- Mandatory security review for all authentication code changes
- EU Digital Services Act compliance by Q1 2025 (currently 65% complete)
- SOC 2 Type II renewal by March 2025 (on track)
- California CPRA updates by January 2025 (80% complete)
- GDPR Data Portability amendment compliance by June 2025

DIRECTIVE 5: COMPETITIVE RESPONSE
Owner: CPO + CTO
Deadline: Ongoing

Actions required:
- Accelerate AI Analytics Platform to General Availability by Q1 2025
- Reduce implementation time from 6 weeks to 2 weeks
  (rapid deployment toolkit in development)
- Leverage SOC 2 Type II and enterprise security as differentiation
  against CompetitorX (InsightAI) and CompetitorY (DataPulse)
- Pricing review: evaluate competitive positioning relative to
  CompetitorX (20% below our list price)

TRACKING AND ACCOUNTABILITY:
- Weekly status updates to CEO office (every Friday by 5 PM)
- Monthly executive review meeting (first Monday of each month)
- Quarterly board presentations for all six investment areas
- KPIs will be tracked in the executive dashboard

Failure to meet stated deadlines will require a written remediation
plan within 5 business days.

— CEO, Adobe Inc.
